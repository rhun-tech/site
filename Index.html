Rhun the Hedgehog was spiky and shy,
His quills on his back reached up to the sky,
But Rhun was humble, not flashy or bold,
He wandered through life, his story untold.
He rolled up in a ball when danger drew near,
His spines a defense, there was nothing to fear,
No need for vanity, no need to show off,
Rhun found peace in his hedgehog trough.
While Hubert the Lion roared loud and wide,
Rhun preferred the quiet of the countryside,
He didn't seek fame or the world's adoration,
Just simple contentment and self-appreciation.
So remember Rhun, the humble hedgehog's tale,
A lesson in modesty, without fail,
In a world full of Huberts, striving for more,
Sometimes being oneself is worth so much more.
